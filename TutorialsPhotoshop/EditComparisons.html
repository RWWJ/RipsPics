EDIT COMPARISONS

This technique will let us compare four adjustments to our image.

Editing topics covered, include:

  • Guide lines
  • Selections
  • Masking
  • Adjustment layers
  • Layer styles (effects)

GUIDE LINES

First we'll setup to make creating the four masks easier.

Make sure in the View menu that Snap is checked (enabled) and that everything in Snap To is checked (enabled)

Lets create two guide lines, one vertically down the center of the image and one horizontally across the center of the image.
These are only guides, they do not show up in your saved image. When we use the selection tool, our selections our will snap to these guides.

Click in the left side ruler area and drag across to the center of the image (aproximate is fine). That should leave a cyan guide line down the center of your image.

Similarly, drag from the top ruler down to center of the iamge.

If all is right, you should have two cyan lines breaking your image into four sections.

SELECTION

Choose your rectangular marquee tool (selection tool) from the Tools panel.

Create the first of four selctions, by clicking in the center of your image (where the guide lines cross) and drag to the upper left corner of the image. If you get close enough to the guide lines, the selction will snap to the lines.

COLOR LUT (Look Up Table)

Add an adjustment layer of Color Lookup.

Do that by clicking the black and white circle icon at the bottom of your Layers panel, then selecting Color Lookup. If you prefer using the menu, it's under Layer, New Adjustment Layer, Color Lookup

When the adjustment layer is added, a mask is automatically created for it using the current selection.

In the Properties window that pops up for the Color Lookup, Try all the different presets until you find one you like (ignore the Load...).

You can close the Properties window to get it out of your way.

STROKE

To visibly seperate our adjustments, we'll outline them.

Add a thick Stroke around this adjustment, by selcting Stroke from the fx Style icon at the bottom of your Layers Panel. Or from the menu (Layer, Layer Styles).

In the Layer Styles window that comes up, change the Size of the Stroke to something in the range of maybe 10 to 30px (not too thick, but thick enough to create a nice border). Feel free to change the Color if you'd prefer something other than black.

Change the Postition to Inside (Outside is the default). This is so our Strokes do not overlap.

The Stroke (and other styles in this window) are applied to the mask of the current layer.

Feel free to try styles other than (or along with) Stroke. For example, Drop Shadow may look good as well.

REPEAT

Now repeat the previous three steps, Selection, Color lookup, and Stroke, three more times, for the upper right, lower left and lower right sections of the image. Each time choosing a different Color Lookup preset for a different look.

FINAL IMAGE

To see the final effect, pres Ctrl-; (or Cmd-; on Mac) to turn off the Cyan guide lines. Or in the menu go to, View, Show, Guides, to toggle off the Cyan guide lines.

Don't forget to save your work!

Aside from practicing a few different editing techniques, this is a good way to be able to compare four different adjustments to your image and see which like best.
